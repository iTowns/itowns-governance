# Code of Conduct — iTowns Project

## Our Pledge

We, as members, contributors, and maintainers of the iTowns project, pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free, respectful, and constructive experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behaviors

- **Be respectful and constructive.** Disagreements happen. Address ideas, not people. Critique code and proposals with evidence and specificity; receive criticism the same way.
- **Be collaborative.** Share context generously. Help others understand your reasoning — in code reviews, issue discussions, and PSC debates alike.
- **Be transparent.** Document your decisions, especially when they affect the roadmap or the governance of the project. Publicly justify significant actions (including contributor exits — see Governance).
- **Assume good faith.** Interpret ambiguous messages charitably. Ask for clarification before escalating.
- **Honor commitments.** If you hold a role (Contributor, Core Contributor, PSC member), meet the activity and participation expectations associated with it. Communicate proactively if you can no longer do so.
- **Respect the roadmap process.** Channel disagreements about technical direction through the established governance mechanisms (Core Contributor review, PSC arbitration) rather than unilateral action.
- **Protect the community.** Report concerns promptly through the channels described below.

### Unacceptable Behaviors

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind — in public or private channels.
- Trolling, insulting, derogatory, or personal attacks.
- Publishing private information about others (doxxing) without explicit consent.
- Sustained disruption of community discussions, meetings, or votes.
- Deliberate misrepresentation of others' positions or intentions.
- Abuse of governance mechanisms — for example, orchestrating votes to exclude a member for strategic rather than legitimate reasons, or manipulating Core Contributor counts to gain disproportionate PSC voting weight.
- Conflicts of interest that are not disclosed — for example, steering the roadmap toward features that exclusively benefit one organization without transparency.
- Any other conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a professional open-source setting.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews)
- The project's communication channels (mailing lists, chat platforms, forums)
- PSC and Core Contributor meetings (in-person or remote)
- Public representations of the project at conferences, events, or on social media

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in external spaces.

---

## Governance Alignment

This Code of Conduct is directly integrated with iTowns' governance rules. Violations may trigger consequences at multiple levels:

| Role | Consequence for CoC Violation |
|---|---|
| **Contributor** | Warning; removal of contribution rights if repeated |
| **Core Contributor** | Warning; vote for removal by other Core Contributors (must be documented and published) |
| **PSC Member (organization)** | Vote for exclusion by other PSC members; loss of seat if Core Contributors from that organization are excluded |

Specifically:

- A Core Contributor may be removed by vote of the other Core Contributors if they fail to respect this Code of Conduct. The decision must be documented, justified, and made public.
- A PSC member organization may lose its seat by majority vote of the PSC if its representatives or Core Contributors persistently violate this Code of Conduct.
- An exclusion at the Core Contributor level can trigger an exclusion at the PSC level if it results in the organization having no active Core Contributor.

These mechanisms exist to protect the integrity of the project and the reputation of all contributing organizations — not to be used as tools for strategic maneuvering. Abuse of exit mechanisms is itself a violation of this Code of Conduct.

---

## Conflict of Interest

PSC members and Core Contributors must disclose any conflict of interest before participating in a vote or technical decision that could benefit their organization disproportionately. Undisclosed conflicts of interest, or attempts to use governance mechanisms for competitive advantage, are considered serious violations.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness a violation of this Code of Conduct, please report it by contacting the PSC at: itowns@ign.fr

Reports can also be made directly to any PSC member you trust if the email channel feels inappropriate for the situation.

All reports will be:

- Treated with confidentiality to the extent possible
- Reviewed by at least two PSC members not involved in the incident
- Responded to within **14 days** with a summary of the steps taken or planned

---

## Enforcement Process

Upon receiving a report, the PSC (or a designated subgroup) will:

1. **Acknowledge** receipt within 72 hours.
2. **Investigate** by gathering relevant information from all parties.
3. **Decide** on an appropriate response, proportional to the severity and pattern of behavior.
4. **Communicate** the decision to the parties involved, with reasoning.
5. **Document** the outcome internally for governance continuity.

Possible responses range from a private warning, to a public statement, to removal from the project in line with the governance rules above.

Decisions may be appealed to the full PSC within 30 days by submitting a written request explaining the grounds for appeal.
